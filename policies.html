NYP Escalation Protocol

In the following scenarios, the attending of record or covering attending must be informed as soon as possible and no later than one hour after awareness.The conversation must be documented in the medical record with an accurate date and time.

  1. Unplanned transfer to ICU or more monitored setting (e.g. SDU)
  2. Unexpected or new-onset hypoxemia requiring supplemental oxygen to maintain O2 sat >90%, or worsening hypoxia requiring escalating ventilatory support (e.g. 100% NRB, BiPAP, C-PAP, or intubation)
  3. Unexpected or new-onset acidosis with pH below 7.30 or Lactate greater than 4
  4. Any new or worsening potentially life-threatening arrhythmia (e.g. ventricular tachycardia, supraventricular tachycardias requiring intervention, bradycardia with hypotension or requiring medication/external pacer placement)
  5. Unexpected new or increasing cardiovascular support (e.g. addition of pressors)
  6. Cardiac arrest, Code, or Rapid Response team called
  7. Development of significant neurological changes (suspected CVA, Seizure, new-onset paralysis)
  8. Iatrogenic event/Serious complications from medical interventions (e.g. ischemic limb)
  9. Unexpected blood transfusion without prior attending knowledge or instruction
  10. Development of any clinical problem that requires an invasive procedure/operation for treatment
  11. New initiation of restraints (4-point restraints)
  12. Signing out against medical advice (AMA) or elopement
  13. Suicide attempt
  14. Death (for patients not on Hospice or other end-of-life care)
  15. At patient, family or other provider request
Inability to reach the attending should NOT impede needed or emergent clinical care. Any member of the team should feel comfortable contacting the attending of record at any time for questions of clinical management.
